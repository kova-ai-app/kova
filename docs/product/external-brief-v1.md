# Kova
## Revenue Intelligence for Drain & Plumbing

## The Short Version

Every plumbing and drain company has a performance problem they can't see. Their best tech closes $1,100 average tickets. Their worst closes $320. The owner knows there's a gap — they don't know why, and they have no way to close it.

Kova records every service call, analyzes what the technician said using AI built specifically for drain and plumbing work, and tells the owner — in dollars, at their actual prices — what was left on the table on every call, by every tech, every day.

Not a coaching score. Not an industry average. The exact dollar amount your tech left on this specific job, at what your company actually charges.

---

## The Problem

Home service businesses lose an estimated 15-35% of potential call revenue to technician behavior gaps. The tools that exist to address this were built for someone else.

**What owners have today:**

- **Ride-alongs.** One manager, one tech, one job. Covers maybe 1% of calls per week. Doesn't scale.
- **Manual coaching programs.** $500-$2,000/month for a human coach who reviews 10% of calls and gives general feedback that isn't tied to what the company actually charges.
- **Generic conversation intelligence (Gong, Chorus).** Built for B2B SaaS reps on Zoom calls. Zero understanding of why a plumber should offer a camera inspection after diagnosing a recurring drain blockage, or what that inspection is worth at this specific company's prices.
- **Rilla and Siro.** The closest competitors — but built for dedicated in-home sales reps doing estimate/close visits, not service technicians performing repairs. Neither product outputs a per-call dollar figure. Neither is connected to the company's pricebook. Rilla has no FSM integration and no plumbing/drain-specific case studies.

The result: owners know their techs vary wildly. They can't diagnose why. They can't fix it systematically. They're paying for coaching that covers a fraction of their calls and can't tell them what a missed camera inspection actually cost in their own numbers.

## The Product

### How It Works

**Before the call:** When a tech is dispatched, Kova sends them a pre-call brief — customer history, job type, and the top 2-3 things to look for on this specific job.

**During the call:** The tech records using the Kova mobile app. One tap. The app works on any iOS or Android phone. Recording is transparent — the tech verbally informs the customer before starting, consistent with recording laws in all 50 states.

**After the call:** Within five minutes of upload, the tech receives a summary on their phone. The owner and manager see the full analysis on their dashboard. Every flagged item is timestamped and linked to the exact moment in the audio.

**The output** looks like this:

```
CALL SUMMARY — Mike Chen | Drain Job | May 5, 2026
Duration: 28 min  |  Overall Score: 61/100

ESTIMATED OPPORTUNITY

  Camera inspection not offered              +$425
  (Your price: $425)
  Customer said "this keeps happening" at 4:22
  [▶ Play 23-second clip]

  Hydrojet upgrade not presented             +$750–$950
  (Your price range: $750–$950)
  Snaking diagnosed, long-term option not offered
  [▶ Play 18-second clip]

  No maintenance plan mentioned at close     +$1,500 LTV
  (Your annual plan: $299/yr → avg 5yr LTV $1,500)
  [▶ Play 31-second clip]

  Total estimated opportunity: $2,890*

WHAT WENT WELL
  ✓ Root cause explained clearly
  ✓ Options-based close attempted

*Based on your pricebook prices. Actual revenue potential depends
 on customer need, timing, and context.
```

That output uses Drain Right's prices — not an industry average, not an estimate pulled from a database. If they charge $425 for a camera inspection, that's the number. If they charge $750-$950 for hydrojetting, that's the range. The owner doesn't have to translate a coaching score into what it means for the business. The translation is already done.

### What's Under the Hood

Kova's AI is built specifically for plumbing and drain — not retrofitted from a generic sales coaching model. It understands the difference between a snake-only diagnosis and one where a long-term hydrojet alternative should have been presented. It knows that "this keeps happening" is a recurrence signal that should trigger a camera inspection offer. It knows what an emergency call looks like and adjusts accordingly — no opportunity flags on a flooding job where the customer needs the water stopped, not an upsell.

The scoring engine runs two layers: a deterministic rules layer that catches specific triggers with certainty, and an LLM layer that catches the nuanced misses — a rushed diagnosis, a close with no options presented, a customer whose concern was never connected to the recommendation. Both layers are fully explainable. Owners can see exactly why a call scored the way it did, down to the timestamp.

### The Pricebook

The pricebook is what separates Kova from every other product in this space. Owners configure their actual service prices — fixed, range, or tiered (good/better/best). Kova ships with California drain and plumbing market defaults so the product is useful on Day 1, with a clear prompt to update each item with real prices as the team gets started. Every dollar figure in every report is tied back to the owner's own pricebook. When prices change, the numbers update.

This is the feature that makes the estimated opportunity number undeniable. Owners already think in dollars-per-truck-roll. A score requires a translation step they often don't take. A dollar amount at their own prices doesn't.

## Why Kova Wins

No competitor does all six things:

1. Records field / in-person calls
2. Applies trade-specific scoring logic for plumbing and drain
3. Uses the owner's actual pricebook to calculate estimated opportunity
4. Integrates with FSMs for invoice verification
5. Is priced for a 3-tech shop
6. Proves its own ROI every month

Rilla is the most-funded, best-known company in the adjacent space. It does none of the last four. It has no FSM connection, no pricebook integration, no trade-specific drain or plumbing logic, and no per-call dollar output. Its pricing is demo-gated and likely $150-300+/seat/month — inaccessible to small shops. And it has a documented technician backlash problem: real Reddit posts from technicians at Rilla companies describing being told to secretly record customers without disclosure, in states where that's illegal.

Kova is built from the ground up the other way. Every recording requires a pre-recording consent prompt that reminds the tech to verbally disclose to the customer before recording begins. The consent event is logged with a timestamp on every call. Techs understand Kova as a tool that helps them earn more and documents their work — not surveillance software their employer deployed without telling them.

> Rilla tells techs they're being watched. Kova tells techs they're leaving money on the table.

The technician adoption problem is real across every product in this category. Kova's answer is framing: recording protects the tech as much as it benefits the owner. When a customer claims a tech damaged something, or said something they didn't say, the recording is the tech's proof.
